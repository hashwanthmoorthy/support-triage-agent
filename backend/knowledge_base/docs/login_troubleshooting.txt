Title: Trouble signing in

If your password is correct but you still cannot sign in, your account may be
temporarily locked after several failed attempts. Locks clear automatically after
15 minutes — waiting and trying again often resolves it without any further
action. Repeated failures within that window restart the timer.

Many sign-in problems are browser-related rather than account-related. An expired
session, a browser blocking cookies, or an outdated saved/autofill password are
the usual causes. Try clearing your browser cache and cookies, disabling
extensions (ad blockers and privacy tools can interfere with the login flow), or
signing in from a private/incognito window. If autofill keeps entering an old
password, delete the saved entry and type it manually.

If your two-factor authentication codes are being rejected, the most common cause
is a device clock that is out of sync. Set your phone's date and time to
"automatic", since time-based codes fail when the clock drifts. If you have lost
your 2FA device entirely, use one of your saved backup codes; if those are gone
too, contact support for identity verification.

If your organization uses single sign-on (SSO), sign in through your company
portal rather than with a password — a "user not found" error usually means you
tried the password form instead of the SSO button. When nothing above works,
note the exact error message and roughly when it started and contact support; a
screenshot of the error speeds up diagnosis. For app crashes or freezes rather
than sign-in errors, see the technical troubleshooting guide.
