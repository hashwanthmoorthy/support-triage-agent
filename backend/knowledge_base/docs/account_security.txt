Title: Account security, 2FA, and suspicious logins

We strongly recommend enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) under Settings >
Security. With 2FA on, signing in requires your password plus a second factor, so
a stolen password alone cannot unlock your account. You can use an authenticator
app (recommended) that generates time-based codes, or SMS codes to your phone.
Authenticator apps are more secure than SMS, which can be intercepted.

When you turn on 2FA we show you a set of one-time backup codes. Save these
somewhere safe and offline — if you lose your phone, a backup code is the fastest
way back into your account. If you run out of backup codes and no longer have
your 2FA device, you will need to go through identity verification with support
to regain access, which takes longer by design.

We monitor for suspicious sign-in activity such as logins from a new device,
country, or an unusual IP address. When we detect one we email you a security
alert and may require additional verification before allowing the session. If you
receive an alert you do not recognize, treat it seriously.

If you believe your account is compromised, act immediately: change your password
from Settings > Security, then open Active sessions and choose "Sign out of all
other sessions" to revoke access on every other device. Review your recent
activity and, if 2FA was not already on, enable it now. Remember we will never
ask for your password or a 2FA code by email, phone, or chat — any such request
is phishing and should be reported to support.
