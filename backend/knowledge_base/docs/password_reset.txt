Title: Resetting your password

If you have forgotten your password or are locked out, you can reset it yourself
without contacting support. On the sign-in page, click "Forgot password", enter
the email address associated with your account, and we will send a reset link to
that inbox. For security we only ever send a link — we never email your existing
or a temporary password.

The reset link is valid for 30 minutes and can be used once. If it expires,
simply request a new one. If the email does not arrive within a few minutes,
check your spam or junk folder, confirm you entered the exact email tied to your
account, and make sure your mail provider is not blocking messages from our
domain. Only accounts that actually exist receive a link, so no email may mean
you signed up with a different address.

When choosing a new password, it must be at least 12 characters and cannot reuse
your most recent password. We recommend a unique passphrase stored in a password
manager rather than something reused from another site.

If your account has two-factor authentication (2FA) enabled, you will still need
your authenticator code after clicking the reset link — resetting the password
does not disable 2FA. If you have lost access to both your email and your 2FA
device, do not keep retrying; contact support to verify your identity through an
account-recovery process. You can also change your password at any time while
signed in from Settings > Security > Change password.
